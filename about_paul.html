About Paul Holmes

Your Local Guide to Leith

Experience Leith through the eyes of someone who knows every corner, every story, and every hidden detail that makes this neighbourhood truly special.

A Life Lived in Leith

Paul Holmes has lived and worked in Leith for over twenty years. In that time, he's witnessed change, challenge, decline, and renewal. But beneath all that movement, he's also seen what endures: the distinctive character of a port community, the voices and stories of its people, and the layers of history written into every street.

His knowledge isn't built from guidebooks alone. It comes from daily walks, conversations at the pub, watching the docks evolve, and noticing the small details that give Leith its character ‚Äî the architecture, the local faces, the hidden corners that most visitors miss.

Paul pays attention. He knows which buildings tell stories of maritime wealth, which lanes were home to working families, and where the old meets the new in surprising ways. He's walked these streets in all weathers and all seasons, and that familiarity shapes every tour he leads.

"This walking tour isn't delivered from a script. It's shaped by first-hand experience and everyday observations."

Why Walk at an Unhurried Pace?

Paul believes Leith is best understood on foot. An unhurried pace allows time to absorb the atmosphere, look around, and connect the past with what's visible today. It's about more than seeing landmarks ‚Äî it's about feeling the place.

He doesn't rush through checklist stops. Instead, he allows space for genuine curiosity, for lingering where something catches the group's interest, and for conversations that reveal layers you wouldn't notice at speed.

The goal is simple but meaningful: that visitors leave with a deeper understanding of Leith, and a sense that they've really been there ‚Äî not just passed through.
